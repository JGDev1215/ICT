External-range liquidity is the real objective; internal-range liquidity is where partials come off. Determine the draw before the entry. [ep06 | Internal vs External Range Liquidity; silver_bullet.html]
Pool quality matters: perfect equal highs/lows and data highs/lows are highest quality; NWOG/NDOG, relative equal highs/lows, and previous session highs/lows are useful B-tier references; standalone order blocks are hit-or-miss as draw liquidity. STEP 2 · SWEEP
Lower-timeframe sweep of opposing liquidity
Price first sweeps external liquidity, then shifts market structure; the displacement that follows is where the FVG forms. [MARKET_MAKER_MODELS | Integration; ep06 | Displacement High & Low]
Opposing pool swept (opposite side to your draw)
Opposing objective 1Primary
Primary opposing pool raided before the entry model.
Opposing objective 2Secondary
Optional secondary opposing pool.
Opposing objective 3Secondary
Optional extended or related opposing pool.
Draws exist on any timeframe — a sweep pool can be a midnight open, PDH/PDL, session H/L, or a gap. The sweep raids the pool opposite your HTF draw; the displacement candle appears on the candle(s) after the sweep, not the sweep candle itself. [ep06 | Bearish ICT FVG; Displacement High & Low]
Use the stated macro windows only; do not invent after-the-hour macro folklore. Bible macro windows: 9:50–10:10, 10:50–11:10, 11:30, 2:50–3:10, 3:15–3:45 [MANUAL_INTERVENTION...MACROS | Macro Time Windows].
Intraday MSS names the short-term draw-on-liquidity shift inside the day. [ep03 | MSS vs Market Structure Break]
