Market Phases
Use these as context labels, not mechanical predictions. ICT framing still begins with the higher-timeframe draw on liquidity and the current dealing range.
Condition 1

Consolidation

Price is contained inside a range, often building liquidity on both sides. The task is to identify the range, its midpoint, and the external liquidity likely to matter when price leaves balance.

Condition 2

Expansion

Price displaces away from a range, liquidity pool, or key level. Expansion matters most when it aligns with the higher-timeframe draw and leaves clear imbalance or displacement evidence.

Condition 3

Retracement

Price returns into a prior dealing range, fair value gap, order block, premium/discount area, or other rebalancing reference. Retracement is not automatically weakness; it can prepare the next delivery leg.

Condition 4

Reversal

Price changes delivery direction after a liquidity objective, failed continuation, market structure shift, or other evidence that the prior directional idea is no longer clean.

How To Apply
StartLabel the condition on the traded timeframe and the higher timeframe separately. Do not force every timeframe to agree.
BiasLet the HTF draw on liquidity dictate directional bias. The phase label only describes the current delivery condition.
ConfirmUse opposing liquidity, timing, MSS/displacement, and the selected entry model to decide whether a setup is actionable.
AvoidDo not treat continuation as a fifth market phase. Continuation is a possible path after context and model evidence line up.
Practical reading: consolidation can lead to expansion; after expansion, price may retrace, reverse, or later consolidate depending on liquidity and order-flow context.
Framework planner: identify HTF draw on liquidity → derive bias from the draw → check opposing sweep/timing context where relevant → apply the selected entry model.
